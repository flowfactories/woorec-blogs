## The Compatibility Crisis: When $75K GMV Meets Cart Abandonment

**Context**: For `LensCraft Pro`, scaling to `$75,000` monthly GMV exposed a critical flaw: `High system switching costs: Customers hesitate to invest in new camera systems due to the high cost of replacing lenses and accessories, leading to abandoned carts.`

At this stage, manual compatibility checks failed. Amateur photographers would spend hours researching lens-camera pairings, only to abandon carts when uncertain about compatibility. The `2 marketing specialists` couldn't keep pace with the 15% return rate caused by mismatched gear, creating a vicious cycle of wasted ad spend and frustrated customers.

## From Rule-Based Guesses to Vector-Driven Precision

To solve this, `LensCraft Pro` deployed **WooRec**.
*Note: Leveraging WooRec SaaS for rapid, low-code implementation without technical staff.*

The transformation wasn't instant. We architected a phased evolution of their recommendation engine:

### Phase 1: Expanding the Candidate Pool
*Powered by WooRec Strategy Module*

We moved beyond static product listings. We implemented a **Hybrid Recall** strategy:

*   **Foundation**: We activated **Hot Retrieval** to surface trending lenses and accessories, solving the Cold Start problem for new visitors.
*   **Advanced**: **Tag-Based Matching** with weighted relevance scoring
    *   *The Logic*: We configured dynamic tag weights (e.g., "Canon EF mount," "f/1.8 aperture," "full-frame compatible") to align user searches with precise technical specifications.
    *   *The Result*: This captured "latent compatibility needs"—finding accessories that technically matched even when users didn't know the exact terminology.

### Phase 2: The Model Evolution (LR to DeepFM)
*Powered by WooRec Model Serving*

To achieve the target `86.7% Conversion Rate surge`, we iterated through three stages:

1.  **The Baseline (Logistic Regression)**: Initially, we used linear models. While fast, they failed to capture complex interactions between camera bodies, lenses, and accessories.
2.  **The Upgrade (DeepFM)**: We introduced Deep Factorization Machines to learn high-order feature interactions (e.g., how "Sony A7 III + telephoto lens + UV filter" combinations perform together), significantly improving accuracy on sparse gear compatibility data.
3.  **The Final State (DeepFM)**: 
    *   *Why this model?*: DeepFM's ability to model feature interactions without manual engineering was critical. It automatically discovered compatibility patterns (e.g., "Nikon Z5 users who buy 50mm lenses also need Z-mount adapters") that rules-based systems missed.

### Phase 3: Traffic Control & Business Logic
*Powered by WooRec Rule Engine*

Raw compatibility scores weren't enough. To align with business goals, we applied a **Traffic Control Layer**:

*   **Diversity (Scatter/Shuffle)**: We implemented a "no more than 2 lenses in a row" rule to prevent visual fatigue and encourage accessory exploration.
*   **Business Injection (Hard Insertion)**: Slot 4 was reserved for high-margin house brand accessories (e.g., LensCraft Pro lens filters).
*   **Dynamic Weighting**: We boosted items based on **Inventory Depth**, ensuring popular in-stock compatible gear (e.g., best-selling Canon RF lenses) appeared first to accelerate fulfillment.
